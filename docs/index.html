Quantitative biology: a quick introduction

Quantitative biology: a quick introduction

Kim Cuddington, Andrew Edwards, Brian Ingalls

2021-11-15

1 Welcome!

This set of independent learning modules covering some important concepts in open science and quantitative biology has been funded by an ecampus grant from the province of Ontario. This is a living document and so revisions to current chapters, and the addition of new chapters, should be anticipated.

The chapters can be used independently (i.e., you don’t have read the whole ebook!). Pick and choose want you need. Each chapter is designed to quickly get you up and running on the topic of interest in 3-5 hours. As such, these materials should be thought of a basic introductions. We’ll provide some pointers to more advanced materials, but our aim is to “jump-start” your research progress with these materials.

On the other hand, the materials are not designed for absolute beginners. Our target audience is graduate students and early professionals in biology. For the open science chapters, we assume you can navigate around your computer and the internet. For the R-based chapters, we assume prior familiarity with R. For material on statistical analyses we assume the standard background in univariate statistics.

1.1 Open Science

In the spirit of open science, all chapters of this book are stored as .Rmd files on github. The whole book can be compiled to various formats using the bookdown package which can be installed from CRAN or Github.

install.packages("bookdown")
# or the development version
# devtools::install_github("rstudio/bookdown")

Remember each Rmd file contains one and only one chapter, and a chapter is defined by the first-level heading #.

To compile to PDF, you need XeLaTeX. You are recommended to install TinyTeX (which includes XeLaTeX): https://yihui.name/tinytex/.
